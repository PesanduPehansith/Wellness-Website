Mental Health

What is mental health?

Mental health encompasses our emotional, psychological, and social well-being, influencing our thoughts, feelings, and actions. It plays a crucial role in how we manage stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices. Throughout life, from childhood to adulthood, mental health is vital. It enables individuals to cope with life's challenges, realize their potential, learn effectively, work productively, and contribute to their communities. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), mental health goes beyond the absence of mental disorders; it involves actively managing conditions and maintaining overall wellness and happiness. Mental illness, on the other hand, refers to a range of conditions that can affect a person's thinking, perceptions, mood, or behavior, making it challenging to handle work, relationships, and other daily demands.



Different types of mental health disorders?

There are more than 200 types of mental health disorders. A few of the most common types of mental health disorders include:

  • Anxiety disorders.
  • Autism spectrum disorder.
  • Depression, bipolar disorder and other mood disorders.
  • Disruptive behavior disorders, such as oppositional defiant disorder and conduct disorder.
  • Eating disorders.
  • Personality disorders, including borderline personality disorder and antisocial personality disorder.
  • Substance use disorders, including drug addiction and alcohol use disorder.


What causes mental health disorders?

Behavioral health disorders can have many causes. They might be the result of abnormal genes. You may inherit those genes from a parent, or genes can change during your life. Chemical imbalances in your brain can also lead to mental illness.

Your risk of mental illness increases if you:

  • Use alcohol or recreational drugs.
  • Don’t get proper nutrition.
  • Don’t have a support system of friends or family members.
  • Have a sleep disorder.
  • Have extreme stress in your life.
  • Have had a traumatic life event or a history of abuse.
  • Struggle with your spirituality or beliefs.


Symptoms of a behavioral health disorder?

Mental health disorders can lead to a broad range of symptoms, including:

  • Use of recreational drugs or alcohol.
  • Avoiding social situations and friends.
  • Changes in sex drive.
  • Excessive worrying or fear.
  • Fatigue or sleep problems.
  • Feelings of sadness or isolation.
  • Intense irritability or anger.
  • Obsession with your physical appearance, weight or eating habits.
  • Problems concentrating, learning or completing everyday tasks.
  • Sudden mood changes, going from “lows” to “highs.”
  • Suicidal thoughts or self-injury.

Behavioral health disorders in children tend to affect their functioning. You might notice:

  • Changes in their performance at school, or how they interact with other children.
  • Disinterest in activities or hobbies they used to enjoy.
  • Excessive worry or anxiety, such as fear of going to bed.
  • Frequent temper tantrums, disobedience or aggression.
  • Nightmares.


Prevention of mental health disorders

Good mental health can be boosted by positive things in life such as:

  • Having support from family, friends and the community.
  • Having a healthy body, by eating a healthy diet and exercising.
  • Reducing stress if possible.
  • being optimistic.
  • developing adaptive ways of coping with life’s problems.
  • getting support.
